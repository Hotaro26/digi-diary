# Small Things, Quietly Beautiful (• ᵕ •)

nature · aesthetics · the ordinary · a recap

---

There's this habit I've been trying to build — actually looking at things.
Not scrolling past them, not half-noticing them. Actually *stopping* and thinking
"wait, that's kind of beautiful isn't it." (˘³˘)♡

These are some of those moments. Nothing dramatic. Just the small stuff that
somehow sticks with you longer than the big things do.

## (*・ω・)ノ On Noticing Things

I think the older you get the faster everything moves. Or maybe it's just that
you stop looking as carefully. (´• ω •`)

When I was younger everything felt worth examining — a weird shaped rock,
the way light hit a dusty window, the texture of an old wall. Somewhere along the
way you start calling those things "normal" and stop seeing them.

This is me trying to unlearn that a little. Pointing a camera at the ordinary
and saying *hey, this was worth remembering.* (｡•̀ᴗ-)✧

<div style="border-left:2px solid rgba(255,255,255,0.2); padding:0.5rem 1.2rem; margin:1.5rem 0; font-style:italic; color:rgba(255,255,255,0.6); line-height:1.8;">
The most beautiful things aren't always the ones people travel thousands of miles to see.
Sometimes it's just the right light at the right moment on a completely unremarkable afternoon.
</div>

No deep meaning here. Just things I saw, thought were kind of beautiful, and didn't want to forget. ♡
